## Release 2 — The Engineer's Console

- Forensic replay — preview-then-commit scrub mode. R2's forensic replay ships as a destructive rewind: clicking a timeline event loads to that tick and the post-rewind state is forfeit. A nicer UX is preview during drag (dashboard shows historical state without touching the live run), with an explicit "rewind here" commit button or a release-to-cancel. That design needs the host to maintain two parallel states (live and scrub-preview) and a query path that can serve historical state without disturbing the active run — a meaningful new architecture. Deferred until destructive scrub proves load-bearing enough to justify the lift; in the interim, Save-before-scrub gives the player a safe-out one click away. #r2-stretch #ui #host
- Phylogeny: lineageTree query response should carry historical extinctionTick. Today the query result has only foundedAtTick; lineages already extinct at the moment of a Load or Rewind come back with extinctionTick=null and render as a dot at their founding tick instead of a true lifeline. Extending LineageTreeEntry with the field would let the phylogeny show a faithful retrospective even after restoring an older save. Out of scope for the R2 phylogeny shipping commit; logged here for future polish. #r2-stretch #protocol #ui
- Phylogeny: long-run row scaling. Each lineage gets one fixed-height row (14 px) and the canvas is sized to fit them all; long runs produce tens of thousands of lineages and blow past the browser's max canvas dimension (~32,767 px in Chrome / Firefox), at which point the canvas paints blank. The MVP caps the rendering at MAX_RENDERED_ROWS (1500) and surfaces a footer with the truncated count, but a real fix would let the phylogeny show "every lineage" via dynamic row height (compress when the population grows), virtualisation (only paint the rows currently in the scrollport), or decimation (skip lineages outside a chosen significance threshold). Pick one when long-run play actually surfaces what the player wants from the view. #r2-stretch #ui
- Phylogeny: rethink legibility from scratch. Even at modest lineage counts the current shape (one row per lineage, branches at speciation, lifelines from foundedAtTick to extinctionTick) is hard to parse — the eye doesn't read it as "how did we get here?" the way a textbook phylogeny does. Player feedback after the first MVP: "still a bit baffling and difficult to read." Brainstorm a better visualisation when the player has time — possibilities include collapsing lineages into clades by drift envelope, using a tree layout that fans out spatially rather than stacking rows, or scaling stroke width with population. The current view ships as a starting point; redesign without ego. #r2-stretch #ui
- Pause button flickers between "Resume" and "Pause" on click sometimes. Click pause; the button briefly shows "Resume" then snaps back to "Pause" while the sim stays paused. Click again; "Resume" sticks. Then "Resume" works as expected. Likely the optimistic flip races a stale heartbeat from before the pause command landed: the heartbeat carries `paused: false` and the rAF coalescer applies it after the optimistic `paused: true` was set. The reconciliation gate (`pausePending`) is meant to defend against this but may not be firing correctly when the flicker is observed. Reproduce with a clean tab, repeated pause/resume cycles; instrument the rAF coalescer with a simTick log to confirm the racy heartbeat hypothesis before fixing. #r2-stretch #ui
- Sim balance: investigate whether resource regen is actually keeping up at long runs. Player observation: substrate map appears fully drawn down after an extended run, yet probes continue replicating. Code reading suggests this is expected — regen scales with cell cap (`cap / 100` per tick at system centres, ~0 toward the edges) and probes coast on stored energy reservoirs across local depletions, so steady-state replication continues at a slower rate. But could be a real bug: integer truncation might silently zero the regen for low-cap cells, diffusion might be eating regen faster than it adds, or replication-cost accounting might be off. Repro: run for 60k+ ticks at 64×, observe the substrate, log substrate-total resources tick-by-tick to see if the curve actually plateaus at a non-zero level. #sim #balance
- Forensic replay: rethink which events deserve to be rewindable. Today the timeline only tracks speciations and treats every one as a first-class rollback target — at fat population that's 10+/sec of low-signal events scrolling past, and the genuinely interesting moments (the extinction of a dominant clade, the patch that propagated to saturation, the first contact, the drift trigger that mattered) are nowhere in the panel. Two-axis design space when this gets picked back up:
  - **(A) What event kinds to surface.** Promote extinction, patchSaturated, autoPaused, firstContact (R3+), treatyViolation (R3+) to first-class rewindable. Keep speciations as a secondary category — filterable, possibly off by default at high speed — so they're available when actually wanted, not drowning the panel.
  - **(B) Whether to filter speciations themselves.** Most speciations are uninteresting (small clades that died quickly). A "significant speciation" is one whose founded clade lasted long or grew to N% of population. Sim-side post-hoc scoring (or a host-side filter on the event stream) would let us promote interesting speciations only. Brainstorm before implementing — there are real questions about whether to score retroactively (and risk hindsight bias) or use a forward-looking heuristic (and risk missing late-blooming clades).
    Either axis would relieve the click-target / aging-out / "wall of L823 → L2146" problems the player surfaced during R2 playtesting. Logged here for a brainstorm, not for direct implementation. #r2-stretch #ui #spec
